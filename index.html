Project One

I receive analysis requests from each of the Third-party Administrators (TPAs), benefit platforms, and Managed Care Organizations (MCOs) that we serve. If there are report analysis requests, the data is pushed to a database. Given SAS's strong reliability in data manipulation, I import, analyze, and generate reports for these organizations. I leverage Tableau for visualization.

In this project, I analyzed a sample of such requests. The request is to conduct a volume-based fraud/waste detection analysis that identifies the intensity of service utilization per patient, without referencing dollar costs. In addition to using volume-based indicators to identify potential fraud, I developed indicators that flag cases requiring further investigation, signaling when providers warrant closer examination beyond just counts and volume patterns. The finalized view of the Service Overurilization and Risk dashboard is shown below.

Healthcare's multifaceted nature requires a sophisticated approach to identifying providers with potential waste or fraud based on high referral counts. As such,this dashboard is powered by multiple variables that combine the volume, time required, and type of healthcare services provided, enabling stakeholders to make informed decisions that guide resource planning, monitor service patterns, identify overutilization, and enhance patient care. The main chart is a scatter plot a scatter plot displaying patients‚Äô risk scores against the number of referrals, each circle and and its size represent the care provider and the time elapsed from diagnosis to each circle representing the time elapsed from diagnosis to seeing a specialist. A detailed overview of the scatter plot is presented subsequently. The sub chart is a state-level view of average referral counts helps identify regional anomalies/hotspots. The table at the bottom (detail section), comprising five fields, enables drill-down analysis for each provider. On the top right-hand side, a filter button allows users to subset the data based on five variables. The following table lists and defines the KPIs.

KPI Definition
Service Intensity Index Offers insight into the intensity and depth of care provided
Elderly Patient Targeting Index A high EPTI highlights providers with unusually high proportions of elderly patients, which may indicate a focus on vulnerable populations.
COPD Patient Targeting Index Measures the proportion of COPD patients a provider sees compared to their total patient population.
Erroneous Billing Rate Number COPD patients billed for rehabilitation with no prior spirometry.
COPD Rehabilitation Utilization An indication of the rate at which patients are empowered to better manage the disease.

The chart above enables comparison of care providers across three dimensions: patient risk, referral risk, and the duration between diagnostic and consultant visits. Providers are classified into four distinct categories, namely:

  • High average patient risk score High average referral count score
  • Low average patient risk score High average referral count score
  • Low average patient risk score Low average referral count score
  • High average patient risk score Low average referral count score

The focus here is on the top-left section of the plot. When severity amplifiers such as duration between diagnostic and consultant vistis, if spirometry was performed before rehabilitation and how long was the rehabilitation session is are applied in the dashboard, we gain deeper insights into the providers with high average referral count while working with patients with low risk. This enables stakeholders to flag providers that may require additional support so as to prevent network abuse and preemptive scheduling.
